Descriptions

Typically applied where individual has risen to the occasion, significantly overachieving in a demanding situation and stands out as a role model, very few individuals are likely to operate at “Outstanding” year on year.

An individual whose approach and quality of work always meets the agreed objectives, expectations and requirements of the role, and often goes beyond.

Performance is in line with most or all the agreed objectives, expectations and requirements of the role. Overall performance is of good quality and at the standard typically expected of the role for most or all areas.

An individual who meets few or only some of the agreed objectives , expectations and requirements of the role. Performance in Key and / or a number of​ aspects is below the standard typically expected of the role and a Performance Improvement Plan is required for the individual.

WHAT objectives

Measures “what” an employee achieves during the performance year

During Objective Setting, You set up individual objectives (“Specific,
Measurable, Achievable, Relevant / Result-oriented, Time-Bound”) that are
aligned with corporate / functional / team objectives, and agree with your
manager on the weighting of each objective.

Specific

Who, What, Where, When, Why, Which
Clearly describe your Objective as much as possible with no ambiguous language.
Who is involved?
What do I want to accomplish?
Where and When will it be done?​
Why am I doing this (reason, purpose)?
Which constraints / requirements do I have?

Measurable

From and To
Make sure you can track the progress and measure the outcome. If your Objective is not quantifiable​, it should be least be verifiable.
How much?
How many?
How will I know when my Objective is accomplished?

Achievable

How
Make sure your Objective is not out of reach or below standard performance.
Is the Objective reasonable enough to be accomplished?
How so?

Relevant

Worthwhile
Make sure your Objective is worthwhile and meets your needs.
Does the Objective contribute and align with company / department plans?
Does the Objective relate to your job and fits with your immediate and long term career plans?

Time-Bound

When
Your Objective should be achieved within a certain and clear timeframe. It will establish a sense of urgency and prompt you to have better time management.

Steps

Identify 1 to 2 development goal(s) which will make the most significant impact in the current Role, and​ accelerate significantly your personal and professional career.

Define what success looks like for these Development Goals: provide very specific and observable behaviors or measures so that progress can be easily captured.

Develop action plans based on the 70/20/10 Model​
Action plan – on the job experience (70%)​

Action plan – Support from Others (Mentoring & Coaching) (20%)​

Action plan – Learning & Training (10%)​

Example

Develop my ability to engage, motivate and influence people during presentations and meetings.

I am able to present to a group of people and, in a succinct and clear manner, provide an inspiring and engaging reason for them to act. They react positively and with enthusiasm to my ideas.

  • I will provide quarterly progress meetings to the team and highlight the successes for the month and the focus for the next month.
  • I will ask trusted members of my team to give me feedback.
  • I will work with the GM and prepare his monthly town hall meetings so that I can plan and review approaches for influencing others.

I will ask Frederick to act as my coach/mentor as he is proficient in this area. We will meet monthly to discuss how to utilise different techniques and approaches to engage people. I will ask him to attend some of my meetings and give me feedback.

Discuss with HR opportunities for a course on executive presence and influencing skills. Also watch videos on TED for tips on effective presentation skills.

Overall Guided Rating Calculation​

Your calculated overall score will be rounded to nearest 5% (your guided AIB Individual Multiplier) and will fall within the performance ranges which corresponds to a performance rating (your guided performance rating).
The guided performance rating and AIB Individual Multiplier will form the basis for further calibration.
Example 1: 109% will be rounded to nearest 5% , i.e. 110% , which falls under range of 110% - 125% of Highly Effective rating.
Example 2: 77% will be rounded to nearest 5% , i.e. 75% , which falls under range of below 80% of Needs Improvement rating.
